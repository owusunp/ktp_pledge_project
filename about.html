A Presidential Welcome

Welcome to KTP’s official website! My name is Haley Kahn, and I am a founder of KTP and the presiding president of KTP’s Rho Chapter. I decided to found KTP at Vandy in order to fill a whitespace. I noticed that despite CS being one of the largest and fastest growing majors at Vandy, there lacked a community for tech-oriented individuals. While there existed organizations that focused specifically on programming or niches in computer science, there was little in the way of professional development combined with a social community.

In KTP’s short time as an organization on campus, we have welcomed many new members and held official recruitment events, workshops, speaker events, and we are looking forward to events with recruiters this fall! Being a pre-professional fraternity, we value having amazing opportunities for our members. This means connecting with faculty, networking with other students, and even getting amazing speakers from Fortune 500 companies to come speak and connect with us.

As professional as we are, we also want to build a social organization here on campus! We have hosted really fun events for our members such as Big-Little, bonding activities, study breaks, and a date party! While we wanted to give members opportunities for professional development, building a community of supportive brothers was a big priority when creating this organization.

As president, I hope to leave a lasting impression on the Vanderbilt community. With new KTP chapters popping up around the country, I am excited to help grow KTP both nationally and here at Vanderbilt. I hope you’ll follow our journey and consider joining us!

Why KTP?

Professional Development

Gain access to exclusive workshops, seminars, and networking events that will help you build essential skills and connect with industry leaders.

Networking Opportunities

Expand your professional network by interacting with fellow members, alumni, and recruiters from top companies, paving the way for internships and job placements.

Community and Support

Become part of a supportive brotherhood that fosters personal growth, mutual respect, and lifelong friendships through various social and bonding activities.

Leadership Experience

Take on leadership roles within the fraternity to develop your organizational and management skills, preparing you for future career challenges.

Service and Giving Back

Engage in meaningful community service projects that allow you to give back to Vanderbilt and the surrounding community, making a positive impact.
